What is Startup Weekend Ottawa?

University of Ottawa is proud to host Startup Weekend Ottawa open to the entire startup community!


In an effort to make the event more accessible to students and alumni in the Capital Region, the Faculty of Engineering has subsidized tickets for this special event. NOTE: All uOttawa students that attend the entire event are able to receive a reimbursement for the cost of their ticket.

This is an official Global Entrepreneurship Week event that aims to connect University of Ottawa, and other students across all faculties to encourage and promote entrepreneurship regardless of discipline of study while connecting these students to Ottawa's startup community.

Categories

While the purpose of a Start up Weekend is about the experience, we've added a bit more! We've put together a prize package (including $1,000) for the best team based on the Sunday pitch. In addition, we've created three individual categories that you can choose to compete in - each category comes with an additional $500 prize package. And yes, you can compete in multiple categories!

Social Impact

This category focuses on the area of social entrepreneurship and inspiring socially sustainable models and cause-based businesses. Dare to think differently and be a difference maker. Create sustainable businesses that create a positive social impact! Consider causes like:

  • Environment
  • Poverty & Homelessness
  • Vulnerable communities

Mobile health

Mobile health is a term used for the practice of medicine and public health supported by mobile devices. Mobile Health apps can collect community and clinical health data, deliver healthcare information to practitioners, researchers, and patients, monitor patient vital signs, and directly provision care (via mobile telemedicine).

Makers

This category is focused on building physical prototypes during the weekend. To do this, you will have equipment available (3D printer, laser cutting and other machining) as well as open-source hardware (Arduino, Raspberry). The possibilities of what can be created during the weekend are just about endless!

Our Speaker

Aydin Mirzaee

Director of Product, Survey Monkey

Aydin Mirzaee co-founded Fluidware in 2008 which was responsible for running two very successful SaaS businesses: FluidSurveys & FluidReview. The company was bootstrapped and grew to a $12M run-rate and over 90 people in staff. After 6.5 years, Fluidware was acquired by SurveyMonkey (the world's leading online survey company). Aydin currently serves as General Manager of FluidReview within SurveyMonkey.

Aydin is also the co-founder of FreshFounders, a non-profit organization with the vision to create a community of young entrepreneurs in every city around the world.

Hanan Anis

Hanan Anis is a professor in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Ottawa. She holds an NSERC chair in Entrepreneurial Engineering Design and is also the coordinator of entrepreneurship and innovation in the Faculty of Engineering. Her passion is to help students develop an entrepreneurial mind set and start successful businesses. She has been teaching entrepreneurship, design and innovation for many years. Prior to joining the university, she was the co-founder and Chief Technology Officer at Ceyba, an optical long-haul networking company that employed 250 people at its peak.

Michel Brûlé

President of the board and Anges Québec active member

Mr. Brûlé obtained both his Bachelor and Master’s degrees in Electrical Engineering from the University of Ottawa. For more than 20 years, he has concurrently pursued careers in both academia and business. In the academia sector, from 1978 until 1995, he was an active contributor to the launch of École de Technologie Supérieure at University of Québec as a Professor, a Research Lab director and Research dean. In the business sector, Mr. Brûlé founded and co-founded six technology companies since 1979, two of which were sold in 2000. His greatest success, Voice-over-ATM technology company InnoMediaLogic Inc., was sold in July 2000 in a transaction honored as “Best VC deal in Canada for year 2000.”

Through private VC fund M&M Investments, since 2000 Mr. Brûlé has helped grow start-up companies and created four generations of spin-offs. Mr Brûlé also actively participated in the launch, and in the on-going operations of the very successful Réseau Anges Québec which he chairs since September 2015. Recognized by his peers as an Angel Investor “before his time”, Mr. Brûlé was named Canadian Angel Investor of the Year 2010 by the National Angel Capital Organization. In June 2012, he received from the University of Ottawa an Honorary Doctorate degree.
